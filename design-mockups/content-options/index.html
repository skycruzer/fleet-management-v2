Fleet Office — content & UX direction options

Visual skin is settled (Operations Navy). These five options restructure WHAT pages show and HOW work flows, based on the 2026-06-11 content/UX review (tasks/content-ux-review-2026-06-11.md). They are not mutually exclusive — pick one as the lead direction.

Dashboard rebuild · medium effort

1 · Morning Brief

Dashboard re-led by action: crew-eligibility banner up top, "action required today" queue, deadline column, KPIs become verbs ("2 overdue — act today"). Pages and nav unchanged.
Touches: dashboard widgets only. Lowest risk, immediate daily value.
 Workflow consolidation · high effort

2 · Approvals Hub

Every decision — leave, RDO/SDO, leave bids, registrations — in one queue with a decision panel: crew-impact preview, seniority context, keyboard-driven sequential review.
Touches: requests + leave-bids + registrations pages, nav. Kills the 3-surface fragmentation (the review's #1 finding).
 Certifications rebuild · medium effort

3 · Compliance Cockpit

Certifications page becomes a triage workspace: severity buckets in a left rail (Critical/Attention/Current), bulk actions (email pilots, link to renewal plan), slide-out detail instead of dialogs.
Touches: certifications page only. Best for the FAA-compliance core job.
 Navigation restructure · medium-high effort

4 · Consolidated IA

14 sidebar items → 6 primary. Leave Bids merges into Requests; Rosters + Renewal Planning become a Scheduling hub with capacity heat-map; Analytics + Reports merge; notifications to a header bell; Help absorbs Support.
Touches: nav config, page merges, redirects. Structural cleanup that benefits every other option later.
 Portal rebuild · medium effort

5 · Pilot-First Portal

Portal home rebuilt for a pilot on a phone: roster strip pinned in the header, next-check countdown hero, bid-deadline band, data lists instead of link tiles, "what changed" on requests.
Touches: portal pages only; admin untouched. Highest user-count impact (27 pilots vs ~3 admins).
